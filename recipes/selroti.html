Sel Roti is a traditional Nepali rice doughnut, crispy on the outside and soft on the inside. With its sweet, subtly spiced flavor, it's a beloved treat often enjoyed during festivals and special occasions.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups rice flour
  • 1 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 cup water (or as needed)
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder (optional, for flavor)
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder (optional, for fluffiness)
  • A pinch of salt
  • Oil for frying (vegetable oil or ghee works well)

Instructions(Steps to make):

  1. Prepare the Batter:

    Mix Dry Ingredients: In a large bowl, combine the rice flour, sugar, cardamom powder, baking powder, and salt. Add Wet Ingredients: Gradually add water and milk to the dry ingredients, stirring continuously to form a smooth, thick batter. The consistency should be similar to pancake batter, thick enough to coat a spoon but not too runny. Adjust the water or milk as needed to achieve the right consistency.

  2. Heat the Oil

    Prepare the Pan: Heat a generous amount of oil in a deep frying pan or wok over medium heat. The oil should be enough to submerge the Sel Roti while frying. Test the Temperature: To check if the oil is hot enough, drop a small spoonful of batter into the oil. If it rises to the surface and starts bubbling, the oil is ready.

  3. Shape and Fry:

    Shape the Sel Roti: Traditionally, Sel Roti is shaped by hand. Dip your fingers in the batter and shape it into a ring or doughnut shape directly over the hot oil. You can also use a spoon to drop the batter into the oil and shape it with the back of the spoon. Fry Until Golden: Fry the Sel Roti on medium heat until it turns golden brown and crispy. Turn it over as needed to ensure even cooking. It should take about 2-3 minutes per side. Drain Excess Oil: Use a slotted spoon to remove the Sel Roti from the oil and place it on a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil.

  4. Serve and Enjoy:

    Enjoy Warm: Sel Roti is best enjoyed warm. It can be served on its own or with tea, coffee, or as part of a larger meal.
