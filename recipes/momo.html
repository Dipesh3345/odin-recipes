MOMO are delectable, hand-crafted dumplings that embody the art of simple yet satisfying comfort food. With their tender, steamed dough encasing a flavorful filling of seasoned meat or vegetables, each bite reveals a warm and aromatic delight.

Ingredients:

For the Dough:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup water (or as needed)

For the Filling:

  • 1 cup finely chopped meat (chicken, pork, or beef) or vegetables (cabbage, carrots, mushrooms)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground coriander
  • Salt to taste
  • Chopped fresh cilantro or green onions (optional)

Instructions(Steps to make):

  1. Prepare the Dough:

    Mix Flour and Salt: In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add Water: Gradually add water while kneading the dough until it becomes smooth and elastic. You may need a little more or less water. Rest Dough: Cover the dough with a damp cloth or plastic wrap and let it rest for at least 30 minutes.

  2. Prepare the Filling:

    Cook Aromatics: Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and ginger, and sautÃ© until fragrant. Cook Meat or Vegetables: Add the chopped meat or vegetables to the pan. Cook until they are fully cooked and any liquid has evaporated. Season: Add soy sauce, black pepper, cumin, coriander, and salt. Mix well. If using, add chopped cilantro or green onions. Cool Filling: Allow the filling to cool before using it.

  3. Assemble the MOMO:

    Roll Out Dough: Divide the dough into small balls. Roll each ball into a thin circle on a floured surface. Aim for about 3-4 inches in diameter. Add Filling: Place a spoonful of filling in the center of each circle. Shape momo: Fold the dough over the filling to form a half-moon shape. Pinch the edges to seal, or pleat the edges to create a traditional momo shape.

  4. Cook the Momo:

    Prepare Steamer: Line a steamer basket with parchment paper or cabbage leaves to prevent sticking. Bring water to a boil in the steamer pot. Steam momo: Place the momo in the steamer basket, making sure they are not touching each other. Steam for about 10-15 minutes, or until the dough is cooked and the filling is hot.

  5. Serve Your delicious Momo with anything you like and enjoy Your day
